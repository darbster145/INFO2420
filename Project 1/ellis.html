Website Critique


Questions


  1. What was your first impression of this site?

    2. Answer: The site feels professional but also personal, like it’s trying to connect with you but also is trying to move you emotionally

  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)

    3. Answer: There’s a menu at the top, and you click around to get where you need to go. No weird layouts or confusion.

  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?

    4. Answer: It’s easy to follow and makes sense. You can find what you’re looking for without digging around.

  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?

    5. Answer: The design is clean and easy on the eyes. It only has 2 pictures on the main page so it is clean. It uses just enough images to keep it interesting but not overwhelming.

  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?

    6. Answer: It loaded pretty fast for me, a second at the most

  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?

    7. Answer: The text is readable, and the background doesn’t distract from it. Nothing fancy, but it works.

  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?

    8. Answer: There’s a nice little menu at the top that’s easy to use. You won’t get lost.

  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?

    9. Answer: The colors and design stay the same across all the pages, which makes it feel polished.

  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?

    10. Answer: It works great on smaller screens too. You don’t have to scroll sideways or zoom in. All of the elements scale down really well.

  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

    11. Answer: It stands out because it feels personal, and the content is relatable and easy to connect with. You can tell thought went into it.
